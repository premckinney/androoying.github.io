About Me

I'm a second-year Computer Science major at Northwestern University in Evanston, Illinois. I've been building for the web since the age of seven, when I published the first iteration of this site using the Netscape WYSIWYG composer and enough tables to organize the federal budget.

As a researcher and designer, I aspire to build systems capable of empowering and educating communities to overcome social barriers. Iâ€™m interested in UX design, human-computer interaction (HCI), social computing, learning sciences, and accessibility.

Through the Design, Technology, and Research program, I am lucky to work with the wonderful folks at Northwestern's Delta Lab. My current work integrates situated learning and crowdsourcing methods to learnersource software development tasks. By completing these tasks, students gain authentic practice while fulfilling real-world programming needs.

Outside of school, I'm a classical flutist and dog lover. I also coach and compete in policy debate, a fast-paced research-based activity. The Northwestern Debate Society is the top collegiate policy debate squad in the nation, and the team's unyielding ethos of character, commitment, teamwork, and hard work has shaped my undergraduate experience more than anything else.
